## Gene Summary
MAP3K5, also known as ASK1 (apoptosis signal-regulating kinase 1), is a member of the mitogen-activated protein kinase kinase kinase (MAP3K) family. These kinases are involved in signaling pathways that regulate cellular processes such as apoptosis, inflammation, and differentiation. MAP3K5 specifically activates the JNK (c-Jun N-terminal kinase) and p38 MAP kinase pathways, primarily in response to stressors such as oxidative stress, endoplasmic reticulum stress, and cytokines. Its expression is ubiquitous, but notably higher in tissues like the heart and skeletal muscle where stress responses are crucial.

## Gene Drugs, Diseases, Phenotypes, and Pathways
MAP3K5 plays a significant role in the cellular response to stress, influencing both apoptosis and survival. Dysregulation of MAP3K5 signaling is implicated in a variety of diseases, including neurodegenerative diseases, cardiovascular diseases, and certain cancers. For example, MAP3K5's activity in promoting apoptosis through its signaling pathways make it a target of interest in cancer treatment, where inhibition of apoptosis is a hallmark. This gene is involved in several critical cellular pathways including the TNF signaling pathway, the Toll-like receptor signaling pathway, and pathways in cancer.
